Insight into the warning signs of Dyslexia

Dyslexia can be discovered as early as Kindergarten and may go undiscovered into adulthood. When an individual has 3 or more of the following warning signs, it is time to discover more about Dyslexia.

Early Signs

  • Talking late
  • Immature speech behind that of peers
  • Frequent ear infections
  • Unable to enjoy rhyming words
  • Word retrieval difficult, often uses: it, thing, that, those
  • Late establishing dominant hand

Sequence and Direction

  • Sequence of alphabet can be challenging
  • Confusing learning days of week, months of year
  • Confuses before and after, yesterday and tomorrow
  • Wearing Velcro shoes past second grade
  • May not have settled on a dominant hand
  • Difficulty telling time on a clock with hands
  • Confuses b–d, p-q, b-p, m-n, j-q, g-q

Speech

  • Multisyllable words can be difficult
  • Poor articulation
  • Verbal communication is consistent with peers, but inconsistent with ability to write
  • Word retrieval difficulty when speaking

Reading

  • Slow, choppy, inaccurate reading:
    • guesses based on shape or context or picture clues
    • skips or misreads prepositions (at, to, of)
    • ignores suffixes
    • can't sound out unknown words
  • Often can’t read high-frequency sight words
  • Poor comprehension of text
  • Difficulty reading printed music
  • At restaurants orders what others order or uses pictures

Spelling and Writing

  • Often writes the wrong homonym
  • Terrible spelling
  • Often can't spell high-frequency sight words
  • Difficulty copying off the board and near-point:
    • can only copy one or two letters at a time
    • often loses place when copying
    • makes mistakes when copying
  • Extreme difficulty taking notes during lectures
  • Difficulty putting thoughts onto paper
  • Letter or number reversals past 2nd grade

School

  • Trouble with math
    • memorizing math facts or multiplication tables
    • memorizing a sequence of steps
    • directionality
  • Homework takes forever; nightly homework wars
    • is unable to do homework without assistance
  • Extremely messy backpack and desk
  • Dreads going to School
    • complains of stomach aches or headaches
    • may have nightmares about school
  • Unable to master a foreign language

Unexpected

  • Otherwise smart, capable, talented
  • Crafty at finding workarounds
  • Strong verbal ability
  • Callenges can be invisible to most everyone else

Family

  • Close relatives struggle with reading, spelling, and writing
  • Frequent job changes
  • Extremely messy bedroom
